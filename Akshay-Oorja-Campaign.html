Flagship Case Study

Akshay Oorja Campaign

Installing roof solar cells and clean tube lightings across Southern Rajasthan's legally isolated tribal pockets.

The Challenge

Communities in the Dark

The tribal pockets near Kaya Village-Vandra Phalla are located inside reserve forest areas. Because of forest preservation laws, layout of standard physical electric grids is prohibited. As a result, families have lived in physical darkness for a century, depending on toxic kerosene lamps and fire hazards.

This causes respiratory illnesses, prevents local children from studying after sunset, and increases vulnerability to local wildlife path dangers at night.

The Hardware Solution

Independent Solar Kits

We supply standalone household solar setups consisting of rooftop modules, Lithium-ion storage cells, double LED lights, and mobile charging ports. This allows families to generate and manage clean power without grid connections.

Rooftop Module

15W / 18V Polycrystalline

Battery Pack

3.2V 6000mAh LiFePO4

LED Tubes

Double 5W / 12V lighting

Device Charger

5V USB Output

Implementation Roadmap

Phased Rollout Strategy

Phase 1

Emergency Safety Lighting

Distributing highly portable solar lanterns and portable battery packs to vulnerable families to handle immediate night safety risks.

Phase 2

Temple & Public lighting

Installing micro-grids on communal gathering trails, village schools, and local village temple squares to establish safe public circles.

Phase 3

Household Solar Autonomy

Installing double LED tubes and fixed panels on roofs of all homes in the forest sections, establishing fully independent setups.
